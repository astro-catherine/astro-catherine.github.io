Introduction

I am a JPL Postdoctoral Fellow in the Exoplanet Exploration Program office. I also work at the NASA Exoplanet Science Institute at Caltech/IPAC. My research focuses on characterizing planets in multi-star systems.

I began my academic career at the University of Michigan (go blue!), where I completed my Bachelor of Science in Astronomy and Astrophysics, as well as in Spanish.

In 2017, I moved to Flagstaff to pursue my PhD in Astronomy and Planetary Science at Northern Arizona University. I worked as a Graduate Research Assistant at Lowell Observatory, where I investigated M-dwarf multiplicity and its effect on planetary system architectures and occurrence rates using high-resolution imaging.

My Research Interests

My research is focused on the intersection between stellar astrophysics, exoplanet science, and astronomical instrumentation. In particular, I seek to characterize multi-star systems and the planets they host in order to understand how these system architectures and occurrence rates differ from single star systems. The Sun Is Lonely

While our Solar System consists of a number of astronomical bodies, we have only one star: the Sun. However, that is not the case for all of the star systems in our galaxy. We believe that about half of solar-type stars in the Milky Way have a stellar companion. There may even be an octuple star system out there! The Search for Tatooine

In order to search for these Tatooines, I use a technique called the radial velocity method. Orbiting planets cause stars to wobble in space, changing the color of the light astronomers observe. I use spectrographs like PARVI to observe this wobble, which allows me to detect planets and to measure their masses. Ultimately, these measurements help me understand how Tatooine-like planets differ from those like Earth!

About Me

I was born in Denver, Colorado, and lived there until I moved to Michigan to begin my undergraduate studies. I have always had a fondness for the mountains, and I love that I can ski and hike in Pasadena, just like at home. The San Gabriel Mountains are ALMOST as beautiful as the Rocky Mountains!

I also have a love of photography, and have tried my best to image the beautiful scenery that has surrounded me over the years. Recently, I have delved into the world of astrophotography. Take a look around my website to see some of these images!
